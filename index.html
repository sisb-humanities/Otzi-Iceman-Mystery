Ötzi the Iceman

The Iceman
A frozen body in the mountains — and a death 5,300 years ago
Grade 7 · English level B1 · History Mystery Investigation

The case

In September 1991, two hikers were walking high in the snowy Alps, on the border between Austria and Italy. They saw something brown sticking out of the ice. It was a human body.

At first they thought it was a climber who had died a few years before. They were very wrong. When scientists studied the body, they got a shock: this man had died about 5,300 years ago. The ice had kept his body, his clothes, and even his tools almost perfect.

People named him Ötzi. But one big question remains: how did he die, so high in the mountains, so long ago? That is your case. You are the investigators.

Case Closed
Ötzi was murdered.

About 5,300 years ago, someone shot Ötzi in the back with an arrow while he was high in the mountains. The arrow cut a major blood vessel and he died quickly. He is often called “the world's oldest murder mystery.”

What really happened

Ötzi was ambushed. An arrow shot from behind hit him in the left shoulder and cut an artery, and he bled to death within minutes. The cut on his hand suggests he had been in a fight a day or two earlier. The killer seems to have pulled out the wooden arrow shaft — but left the stone tip inside, where it stayed hidden for thousands of years. We still do not know who killed him, or exactly why. But we know how he died.

How the case was solved

For ten years the murder stayed hidden. Then, in 2001, a CT scan revealed the stone arrowhead buried in his shoulder. Modern science also read his last meal, studied his clothes and tools, and even tested his DNA — finding he had brown eyes and relatives still living in the Alps today.

What people used to think

At first, everyone believed Ötzi had simply been caught in bad weather and frozen to death. It was a sensible guess that matched what they could see — but it was wrong. The arrow that killed him was hidden deep inside his body, where no one could see it until new technology arrived.

⚠ The red herring

The blood from other people on Ötzi's weapons is the red herring. It is real and fascinating — it suggests he had been in a fight, or perhaps carried a wounded friend — but it does not tell us who killed him. The clue that solves his death is the arrow in his back. Don't let an exciting clue pull you away from the question you are actually trying to answer.

The big thinking point

For ten years, careful scientists believed the wrong thing — not because they were lazy, but because they could not see the hidden clue. When a new machine arrived, the answer suddenly appeared. So how sure can we ever be that we are “right”? Should we always be ready to change our minds when better evidence comes along?
